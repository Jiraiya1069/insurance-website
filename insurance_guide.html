üìÑ Introduction to Insurance

Insurance is a financial product sold by insurance companies to safeguard you and/or your property against the risk of loss, damage, or theft. In exchange for a premium, the insurance provider promises to pay for specific financial losses during the term of the policy.

Insurance acts as a safety net. It offers peace of mind, knowing that in the event of an unfortunate event, you will not bear the entire financial burden alone. It helps individuals, businesses, and governments manage risk and recover faster from losses.

üíº Life Insurance Explained

Life insurance ensures that your family or chosen beneficiaries receive a lump sum upon your death.

  • Term Life: Low-cost, fixed-term policy (e.g., 20 years)
  • Whole Life: Lifelong protection with a savings component
  • Endowment Plan: Combines insurance and savings

üè• Health Insurance Explained

Health insurance pays for medical costs due to illness, injury, or preventive care. It may include doctor consultations, hospitalization, surgeries, diagnostics, and prescriptions.

Key factors:

  • Sum insured
  • Cashless network hospitals
  • Pre-existing condition coverage
  • Waiting periods
  • Co-payments and exclusions
